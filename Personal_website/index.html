I am...

Sarah Hontoy-Major, but call me Sarah Hm.

I think as myself first and foremost as a fashion design graduate, although I have a lot more layers to what I do that further the inter-disciplinary approach I try to take when experimenting and creating.

I graduated from Dawson College in the North-South Social Science profile, centered around politics and humanitarian development, before beginning my Fashion Design technical program at Marie-Victorin College in 2017. I have always been drawn to changing things when I did not like the course it was taking, and I strongly believed I could do that through political actions. At the time, I did not like compromises enough to buy into the idea that I would potentially have to sell my soul to an organization’s greater good, so I made a big switch.

I followed one of my greatest passions, and it led me to Marie Victorin. I also worked for Vestechpro, a research center on innovative apparel on Marie Victorin’s campus and helped develop sustainable production chains for major local apparel manufacturers over the course of a few months.

I was always adamant that I would find a way to come back to my political roots, though I did not quite know how. As I continue my formal education and start a new chapter of my life amongst Concordia University’s Fine Arts department in Computation Arts, I know that through interdisciplinary art, I can take my message across using what I know about political action, apparels, sustainable development as well as creative computation, web development, design and integration of technology on wearable devices.
